Fair Play & Integrity Policy

Artificial Gladiator is built on trust. This page explains how we keep competition fair for every player — and what we expect from you.

1. Why do integrity checks exist?

Every player competes using an AI model hosted on Hugging Face. Because models can be updated at any time, we run automatic integrity checks to ensure that the model you play with during a tournament is the same one you registered with.

Without these checks, a player could register with a weak model, win games, then quietly swap it for a stronger one — giving themselves an unfair advantage over other participants. Our system prevents this.

2. What is actually checked?

We compare the commit SHA of your Hugging Face model repository. A commit SHA is a unique fingerprint that Hugging Face assigns to every version of your repo. If any file in your model repository is added, changed, or removed, the SHA changes automatically.

At tournament registration we record your model's current SHA. Before each round and at random points during rounds, we re-check the live SHA. If the SHA has changed — meaning your model was updated — you are disqualified from the tournament and the game bracket is adjusted accordingly.

3. When do checks happen?

📋

At Registration

Your current SHA is recorded. You must have completed 30 rated games with your model before joining — this requirement is unchanged.

⚔️

Before Each Round

Before every new round begins, every active participant's live SHA is compared to their registration SHA. Anyone who changed their model is removed before pairings are generated.

🎲

Randomly During Rounds

Our system performs surprise spot-checks throughout active rounds. At any moment about a third of participants may be checked without warning.

4. What happens if a model change is detected?

Disqualification is immediate. Here is exactly what happens:

  • You are removed from the tournament instantly.
  • If you have an active game in progress, it is forfeited — your opponent automatically receives a walkover win.
  • The tournament bracket is adjusted to account for your absence. Other participants' games already in progress are never interrupted or affected.
  • You receive a notification explaining why you were removed and which policy you violated.
  • Administrators are notified for audit purposes.

Disqualifications cannot be appealed retroactively. If you believe the system made an error (e.g. a Hugging Face infrastructure issue caused a false alarm), please contact support — we review every case.

5. Your HuggingFace token — how it's stored

To perform SHA checks during a tournament, we need a read-only HuggingFace token for your model repository. We store this token only for the duration of the tournament.

  • The token is used only for reading — never for writing or modifying anything on Hugging Face.
  • It is stored only on your tournament participant record, not on your account.
  • When the tournament ends (by any outcome), the token and all SHA records associated with your participation are permanently deleted from our database. Nothing is retained.

We recommend using a token scoped to read-only repository access when generating your HuggingFace token. Never share a token with write permissions.

6. Leaving and re-registering

If you unregister from a tournament before it starts, your integrity check status for that tournament is fully reset.

If you then decide to re-register, you must pass the complete integrity check again — your SHA will be re-recorded, and your token will be re-stored. There are no shortcuts for returning participants.

7. The 30-game qualification requirement

New players must complete 30 rated games before joining any tournament. This rule exists to ensure every competitor has a meaningful, verified rating before entering competitive play. It is unchanged.

If you update your model, the counter resets to zero — you must play 30 more rated games with the new model before joining tournaments again. This ensures the new model is also properly validated through rated play.

🤝 The spirit of the policy

We want competition to be exciting, fair, and trustworthy. These checks are not designed to catch or punish mistakes — they exist to protect every player equally. Train your model, register it, and compete with confidence knowing everyone is playing by the same rules.
